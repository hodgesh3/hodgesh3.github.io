Scheduling Appointments

Routine Appointments such as annual check-ups, DOT physicals, and follow-up visits can all be scheduled in advance by calling our office or sending us a request through the patient portal.

New Patient Appointments will be scheduled once we have recieved your past medical records. For details about this process and the forms necessary to establish as a new patient, click here.

Same Day Appointments are available to established patients because illness and injury cannot be anticipated. Thirty percent of our appointment slots are reserved for same day scheduling, so give us a call when you need us. These slots fill up quickly, so call as early in the day as possible!

After a Hospitalization: for a smooth transition after a hospitalization or nursing home rehabilitation, Dr. Matheson would like to see you within 7 days of your discharge. We reserve time in our schedule for these appointments as well, so please contact us when you are discharged to home.

No appointment necessary for:

  • Routine injections such as allergy shots, vitamin B12 or testosterone injections, flu and pneumonia shots, or TB tests
  • PT/INR lab test for anti-coagulation management
  • Blood Pressure checks or urinalysis

Prescription Refills

Please contact your pharmacy when you need refills, or use your Patient Portak to send us your refill request. Submit your request at least 24 hours before you need your medication, or longer if you are making your request over the weekend.

URGENT CARE

Call 911 if you have symptoms of chest pain, shortness of breath, a change in mental status, uncontrolled bleeding or inability to walk.

For less severe symptoms, you may not always know if it is better to go to a hospital emergency department, urgent care center, or if you should contact Dr. Matheson first. Here are some guidelines to help you navigate that decision. Whenever you need health care, please call our office first (231-757-2500). If the office is open we can help by either scheduling you to be seen that day, or by providing triage and treatment recommendations over the phone. If the office is closed, a recorded message will give you instructions including how to get in touch with us after hours so that we can make a decision together about whether or not you need to go to the hospital. (The after-hours call center is 231-843-2557. They will page Dr. Matheson and he will call you back.) The only time we would not want you to call the office first, is if the circumstances require a call to 911 so that an ambulance can attend to you immediately. Examples of this type of circumstance would be: symptoms of a stroke or heart attack, loss of consciousness, trauma resulting in uncontrolled bleeding or inability to walk.
